    I'd previously owned two pairs of IE80 which were different to this S
version in terms of build, hence only 3 stars this time. I'll get to that
shortly but first a short description of what we get.These are in-ear buds of
the type that go right into the ear canal and need to be tightly fitted in order
to deliver audio as intended. There are different sized tips supplied in order
to offer a good range of fit but nothing like the range that sometimes comes
with earbuds. They can be bought separately though very cheaply. There is a
fabric-covered zip case which is very sturdy and securely holds the IE80 S if we
take the time to store them away properly. It can also hold some spare tips and
a cleaning tool and bass-adjuster. There is a net pouch on one side for storing
other items. The case is practical but doesn't look as impressive as the silver
plastic case that came with the IE80. It might be longer lasting though.Some
might be wondering how in-ear buds can cost so much. What's so good about them?
Well, in short, it's the sound quality. When properly seated in the ear canal
with a good audio source (for example a flagship phone with a proper audio app
like Poweramp, playing FLAC files) one can really hear the difference. There is
audible detail that just isn't there with other headphones. As well as adjusting
it from source, the bass output of the IE80 S can be adjusted by turning a small
dial on each bud. There is a tool included for doing this. I tend to set mine
somewhere in the middle as I find that turning it all the way up sets it too
heavy at the expense of middle frequencies, and too far down sounds a bit weedy,
as it did with the original IE80. When I first encountered this system I thought
it was a gimmick but it does change the output of the earbuds significantly.
I've found the audio quality of the IE80 S to be no better or worse than the
IE80, but I suspect that it has been improved to a degree.The IE80 S is inferior
to the model it replaces. The build quality, more specifically the choice of
materials, is not as good. The wire is thicker and does not seem to recover from
twisting in the same way the IE80 did. It's actually too thick to hang over the
ears correctly and ends up curving above the ear, inviting anything to catch it
and dislodge the bud. It also carries vibration through the wire in a way the
IE80 didn't. Just slightly brushing the wire is very audible. I don't expect
this from Â£200+ earbuds.There is a wire toggle that can be moved up and down to
prevent the earbuds becoming tangled when not in use. A great idea that is now
present on many earbuds. On this toggle is a hologram. (Incidentally, - if there
is no hologram the IE80 S are probably fake; this is a good way to tell whether
or not they are genuine). The hologram on mine has not worn very well and has
quickly become half blackened. Even worse, the logo on the outside of each bud
is painted on and has already started to wear away on each bud. Nothing like
this ever happened with my IE80. The bent wire, blackened hologram and
disappearing paint make them look a bit shoddy and I've only had them for 2
months. I'm very disappointed with their appearance and that they are not as
comfortable to wear. The IE80 also had a good right-angle 3.5mm jack. The IE80 S
has a cheap-looking straight one which won't be as durable.Despite their amazing
sound quality I would not buy these again because they are so much worse than
the model they are replacing. The build quality is certainly not worth the
asking price.Sound quality is 5 stars. Build quality 2 stars. Overall value 3
stars.UPDATE: The silly thick wire failed at one earbud after only 3 months, see
photograph. Reduced from 3 stars to 2. I wasn't expecting to have to buy a
decent replacement wire so soon. Sennheiser, this is a substandard product.2ND
UPDATE: I bought myself a genuine IE80 wire, which works fine with the S. Things
are much better now, but it cost me Â£35. I don't know why Sennheiser didn't
continue with the IE80 wire.
